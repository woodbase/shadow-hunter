# Xeno Breach – Gameplay Systems

Version: 0.1

This document describes the main gameplay systems used in Xeno Breach.

The goal is to define how core mechanics interact so that all systems support the tone of tension, uncertainty, and survival.

---

# 1. Player Movement System

The player movement system should feel deliberate and grounded.

Movement types include:

Walk  
Default movement speed.

Run  
Faster movement but increases noise and enemy detection risk.

Aim  
Slows player movement while improving weapon control.

Movement should feel slightly heavy rather than fast and arcade-like.

---

# 2. Weapon System

Weapons should feel realistic and dangerous.

Design principles:

Limited ammunition  
Meaningful reload times  
Noticeable recoil  
Controlled shooting

Weapons should not encourage constant firing.

Examples of early weapons:

Service pistol  
Basic sidearm with moderate accuracy.

Shotgun  
Short range but powerful.

Compact rifle  
Balanced weapon with controlled recoil.

---

# 3. Ammunition System

Ammunition should be limited.

Players should not feel comfortable wasting bullets.

Ammo may be found in:

- storage crates
- security lockers
- abandoned equipment
- fallen personnel

Ammo scarcity helps maintain tension.

---

# 4. Infection System

Close contact with infected enemies can expose the player to contamination.

Infection may occur when:

- hit by biological attack
- standing in contaminated areas
- interacting with infected entities

Infection effects may include:

- gradual health loss
- blurred vision
- coughing sounds
- movement impairment

The infection system reinforces the danger of close-range encounters.

---

# 5. Health System

Player health represents physical condition.

Damage sources include:

- enemy attacks
- environmental hazards
- contamination effects

Health recovery may come from:

- medical supplies
- safe areas
- special equipment

Health should not regenerate automatically.

---

# 6. Enemy AI System

Enemy behavior should prioritize tension rather than aggression.

Basic AI behaviors:

Idle  
Enemy is stationary or slowly wandering.

Investigate  
Enemy reacts to noise or movement.

Chase  
Enemy moves toward the player after detection.

Attack  
Enemy performs close-range biological attack.

Enemies should not behave like traditional shooter enemies.

Their behavior should feel erratic and unsettling.

---

# 7. Detection System

Enemies detect the player through multiple cues.

Detection sources include:

Sound  
Running, shooting, or interacting with objects.

Movement  
Visible movement in enemy line of sight.

Proximity  
Player entering close distance.

Stealth and caution should reduce detection risk.

---

# 8. Objective System

The objective system drives player progression through levels.

Common objectives include:

- restoring power
- accessing research terminals
- unlocking sealed doors
- retrieving data modules
- activating containment systems

Objectives should push players deeper into the facility.

---

# 9. Interaction System

Players should be able to interact with the environment.

Common interactions include:

- opening doors
- activating terminals
- collecting items
- triggering scripted events

Interactions should feel grounded in the facility setting.

---

# 10. Breach Anomaly System

Some areas may contain unstable breach phenomena.

Examples:

Spatial distortions  
Biological growths  
Electrical interference  
Reality glitches

These anomalies may:

- damage the player
- alter movement
- distort vision
- trigger enemy behavior

They reinforce the idea that the environment itself is unstable.

---

# 11. Resource System

Players must manage limited resources.

Important resources include:

- ammunition
- medical supplies
- equipment items

Players should often feel uncertain about whether they have enough resources to proceed safely.

---

# 12. System Design Rule Summary

All gameplay systems must support the core experience:

Industrial  
Dangerous  
Unpredictable  
Immersive

Systems should encourage caution and tension rather than constant combat.
