# Xeno Breach – Game Design

Version: 0.1

This document defines the core gameplay systems of Xeno Breach.

The purpose is to ensure all contributors build mechanics that support the same gameplay vision.

---

# 1. Game Premise

Humanity developed experimental technology known as **Breach Drives**.

These drives create microscopic tears in space to allow faster-than-light travel.

The discovery made interstellar expansion possible.

However, space beyond the tear is not empty.

Something exists on the other side.

After several research facilities stopped responding, a private containment contractor was deployed.

You are part of that team.

Your job is not exploration.

Your job is **containment and cleanup**.

---

# 2. Core Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay loop is built around exploration, tension, and survival.

1. Enter breached facility
2. Explore dark industrial environments
3. Locate objectives
4. Avoid or neutralize hostile entities
5. Manage ammunition and resources
6. Contain or escape the breach

The player should constantly feel tension and uncertainty.

---

# 3. Player Role

The player is **not a superhero**.

They are a trained containment operator.

Capabilities:

- firearms training
- tactical movement
- limited equipment

Limitations:

- limited ammunition
- limited visibility
- limited information

Survival depends on caution.

---

# 4. Player Abilities

Player abilities include:

Movement

- walk
- run
- aim weapon
- reload

Interaction

- open doors
- activate terminals
- collect items
- trigger objectives

Combat

- firearms
- limited ammunition
- deliberate shooting

Combat should feel **heavy and dangerous**, not arcade-like.

---

# 5. Combat Philosophy

Combat in Xeno Breach should feel tense.

The player is not meant to fight large numbers of enemies.

Encounters should be:

- unpredictable
- dangerous
- short

Avoid arcade-style combat.

---

# 6. First Enemy Type

The first enemy encountered in the game **does not use ranged weapons**.

These entities were originally human personnel exposed to the breach.

They are infected or altered by an unknown biological agent.

Key characteristics:

- slow but unpredictable movement
- aggressive at close range
- distorted human behavior

Attack method:

The entity attacks at **close range** by releasing a biological contaminant.

Possible effects:

- infection
- damage over time
- impaired vision
- coughing or breathing effects

These enemies are meant to create **tension in close spaces**, not firefights.

---

# 7. Enemy Design Philosophy

Enemies should feel unsettling rather than action-oriented.

Rules:

- enemies should rarely appear in large groups
- encounters should feel sudden and dangerous
- sound design should hint at enemy presence

Players should often hear enemies **before they see them**.

---

# 8. Weapons

Weapons should feel realistic and controlled.

Examples:

- pistol
- shotgun
- compact rifle

Weapon characteristics:

- noticeable recoil
- limited ammo
- meaningful reload times

Spray-and-pray gameplay should not be encouraged.

---

# 9. Level Structure

Levels represent sections of breached facilities.

Examples:

- research labs
- maintenance corridors
- storage sectors
- reactor rooms
- quarantine zones

Each level should include:

- exploration
- environmental storytelling
- tension through darkness and sound

---

# 10. Environmental Storytelling

The environment should tell the story.

Examples:

- abandoned equipment
- blood trails
- broken containment chambers
- emergency lockdown messages
- flickering lights

Players should gradually understand what happened in the facility.

---

# 11. Tension Systems

Tension is central to the experience.

Ways to create tension:

- darkness
- sound cues
- limited visibility
- distant movement
- flickering lights

Enemies should not always appear immediately.

Suspense is important.

---

# 12. Long-Term Design Direction

Future gameplay systems may include:

- motion detection equipment
- contamination mechanics
- alien entities
- unstable breach zones

All future systems must support the core tone of the game:

Industrial  
Dangerous  
Unknown  
Unstable
