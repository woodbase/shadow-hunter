# Xeno Breach – Level Design

Version: 0.1

This document defines how levels and environments should be designed in Xeno Breach.

Levels must support the core experience of tension, exploration, and uncertainty.

Players should feel like they are entering abandoned facilities where something has gone terribly wrong.

---

# 1. Level Design Philosophy

Levels should create tension through environment and pacing.

Key design principles:

- darkness
- uncertainty
- limited visibility
- environmental storytelling
- sudden encounters

Players should never feel completely comfortable.

Silence and atmosphere are as important as enemies.

---

# 2. Facility Structure

Facilities should feel like real infrastructure.

Common areas may include:

- research labs
- storage rooms
- maintenance corridors
- reactor sections
- quarantine areas
- control rooms
- security checkpoints

Rooms should serve believable purposes.

Avoid maze-like layouts that feel artificial.

---

# 3. Level Flow

Levels should guide the player through exploration without obvious linear paths.

Typical flow:

1. Entry area
2. Exploration zone
3. First encounter
4. Objective discovery
5. Deeper facility sections
6. Escalating tension
7. Extraction or containment event

Players should gradually move deeper into the facility.

---

# 4. Environmental Storytelling

The environment should communicate what happened before the player arrived.

Examples:

- blood trails in corridors
- overturned equipment
- damaged containment chambers
- emergency lockdown messages
- flickering lights
- abandoned tools and personal items

Players should piece together events themselves.

Avoid long exposition.

---

# 5. Lighting Design

Lighting is one of the most important tools for level design.

General rules:

- most areas should remain dark
- lighting should create strong shadows
- some lights may flicker or fail

Lighting colors should communicate situation.

Normal facility lighting  
Cold white or blue

Emergency lighting  
Red

Technology or terminals  
Cyan glow

Unknown anomalies  
Distorted or purple lighting

---

# 6. Sound and Atmosphere

Sound should enhance tension and immersion.

Common environmental sounds:

- distant machinery hum
- ventilation systems
- dripping water
- electrical interference
- metal creaking

Enemy sounds may be heard before visual contact.

Silence can also be powerful.

---

# 7. Enemy Placement

Enemies should be placed deliberately.

Avoid large groups.

Preferred encounter styles:

- single enemy in a corridor
- enemy emerging from darkness
- enemy reacting to sound
- enemy appearing after a scripted environmental event

Players should not know when or where the next enemy appears.

---

# 8. Safe Areas

Some areas should temporarily feel safe.

Examples:

- control rooms
- sealed laboratories
- security stations

These areas allow the player to:

- read logs
- interact with terminals
- recover mentally

However, safety should never feel permanent.

---

# 9. Objectives

Objectives should feel natural within the facility.

Examples:

- restore power
- access research terminals
- unlock sealed sectors
- retrieve data cores
- activate containment systems

Objectives should push the player deeper into dangerous areas.

---

# 10. Tension Building

Levels should build tension gradually.

Early areas:

- quiet
- minimal enemies
- exploration

Mid areas:

- first encounters
- environmental warnings
- subtle disturbances

Late areas:

- increased danger
- stronger enemy presence
- unstable environments

The goal is escalation without constant combat.

---

# 11. Visual Landmarks

Players should be able to orient themselves using environmental landmarks.

Examples:

- large reactor chamber
- distinctive machinery
- unique lighting areas
- security gates

This prevents players from feeling lost in similar corridors.

---

# 12. Replayability

Levels should encourage replayability.

Possible methods:

- alternate routes
- randomized enemy positions
- environmental variations
- hidden story elements

The facility should feel slightly different each time.

---

# 13. Level Design Rule Summary

Every level should support these design pillars:

Industrial  
Dark  
Tense  
Unpredictable  
Immersive
