# Xeno Breach – Lore

Version: 0.1

This document describes the narrative background of the Xeno Breach universe.

The goal is to establish the rules of the world so that environments, enemies, and story elements remain consistent.

---

# 1. The Breach Drive

In the late 21st century humanity discovered a revolutionary propulsion technology known as the **Breach Drive**.

The drive allows spacecraft to travel faster than light by creating microscopic tears in space.

Instead of moving through space, ships briefly pass through a small rupture in reality.

This discovery made rapid interstellar travel possible.

At first the technology appeared safe.

The tears always closed.

Until they didn’t.

---

# 2. The First Incidents

Several deep-space research facilities reported unusual anomalies after repeated Breach Drive experiments.

Reports included:

- unexplained biological contamination
- electrical interference
- altered behavior among crew members
- unknown organic growths in sealed environments

Most incidents were classified and contained.

Then an entire research facility went silent.

---

# 3. The Breach Events

When a breach becomes unstable, something from the other side may enter our reality.

These events are known as **Breach Incidents**.

Characteristics of a breach event:

- localized spatial distortion
- biological contamination
- psychological disturbances
- loss of communication
- environmental damage

Facilities experiencing breach incidents are placed under **immediate containment protocol**.

---

# 4. Containment Contractors

Because breach incidents are unpredictable and dangerous, governments began outsourcing containment operations.

Private containment teams were created to:

- investigate silent facilities
- contain biological threats
- retrieve research data
- neutralize contaminated personnel

These teams are not soldiers.

They are **cleanup crews for reality failures**.

---

# 5. The Player

The player is a member of one of these containment teams.

Their mission is simple:

Enter the breached facility.

Determine what happened.

Recover critical data if possible.

Contain the threat.

Or survive long enough to report it.

---

# 6. The Infection

One of the first discovered effects of breach exposure is a biological contamination.

Personnel exposed to the breach may become infected by an unknown organism or viral agent.

Symptoms include:

- aggression
- erratic movement
- respiratory distress
- visible biological distortion
- loss of higher cognitive function

These infected individuals become the first hostile entities encountered in the game.

They cannot use weapons.

Instead they attack at close range and release infectious biological material.

---

# 7. Unknown Origin

It is not known whether the contamination is:

- a virus
- a parasite
- a form of alien life
- a biological reaction to exposure

Some researchers believe the breach opens into an ecosystem that does not follow the same biological rules as Earth.

Others believe something is actively trying to cross through.

No one knows the truth.

---

# 8. Containment Doctrine

Containment teams follow strict doctrine when entering a breached facility.

Primary objectives:

1. Identify the source of the breach
2. Contain biological threats
3. Recover critical research data
4. Prevent spread of contamination

If containment fails, the facility may be permanently sealed or destroyed.

---

# 9. The Unknown Side

Very little is known about what exists beyond the breach.

What is known:

- The space beyond the tear is not empty.
- Biological contamination originates from it.
- Some entities appear to move through the breach intentionally.

Whether this is an ecosystem, a dimension, or something else entirely remains unknown.

---

# 10. Story Philosophy

The story of Xeno Breach is not fully explained through dialogue.

Instead, players learn what happened through:

- environmental storytelling
- abandoned recordings
- terminal logs
- damaged research notes
- visual clues in the environment

Mystery and uncertainty are central to the experience.
