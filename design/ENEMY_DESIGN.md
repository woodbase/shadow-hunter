# Xeno Breach – Enemy Design

Version: 0.1

This document defines the design philosophy and behavior of enemies in Xeno Breach.

Enemies should support the atmosphere of tension, uncertainty, and survival.

The goal is not constant combat but **unpredictable encounters**.

---

# 1. Enemy Design Philosophy

Enemies in Xeno Breach should feel disturbing and unnatural.

Encounters should emphasize:

- tension
- unpredictability
- close-range danger
- psychological pressure

Avoid:

- arcade-style enemy swarms
- large firefights
- enemies that behave like traditional shooter targets

Players should often **hear enemies before they see them**.

---

# 2. Enemy Categories

Enemy types will generally fall into these categories.

Infected Personnel

Former human staff exposed to the breach.

Entities

Unknown organisms originating from the breach.

Anomalies

Reality distortions that behave like living threats.

---

# 3. First Enemy Type – Infected Personnel

The first enemy encountered in the game is an infected human.

These individuals were once facility staff who were exposed to the breach contamination.

They retain limited motor function but have lost higher reasoning.

Key characteristics:

- aggressive behavior
- erratic movement
- distorted breathing
- unpredictable reactions

They should feel tragic and unsettling rather than monstrous.

---

# 4. Combat Behavior

The infected cannot use firearms.

They attack only at close range.

Attack method:

They release a biological contaminant when within close distance of the player.

Possible attack effects include:

- direct damage
- infection buildup
- coughing or breathing impairment
- temporary vision distortion

The attack should feel biological and uncomfortable rather than explosive.

---

# 5. Movement Style

Movement should feel unnatural.

Examples:

- slow staggering
- sudden bursts of speed
- twitching motion
- unpredictable turns

The goal is to create uncertainty in how the enemy approaches the player.

---

# 6. Detection Behavior

Enemies detect the player primarily through:

- sound
- proximity
- movement

Enemies should not immediately detect the player at long distances.

Darkness and caution should benefit the player.

---

# 7. Sound Design

Enemy sounds are extremely important for tension.

Examples:

- distorted breathing
- coughing
- dragging footsteps
- wet organic sounds
- distant movement in corridors

The player should often hear these sounds before the enemy becomes visible.

---

# 8. Encounter Design

Enemies should rarely appear in large groups.

Typical encounters:

- single infected in a corridor
- one enemy emerging from darkness
- an enemy appearing after environmental noise
- enemies approaching slowly from behind

The player should never feel completely safe.

---

# 9. Damage Philosophy

Enemies should be dangerous even in small numbers.

Design goals:

- enemies are threatening at close range
- player must manage distance
- ammunition should feel valuable

Encounters should reward caution rather than aggression.

---

# 10. Future Enemy Types

Later in the game additional enemy types may appear.

Possible examples:

Advanced Infection

Heavily mutated personnel with increased strength.

Breach Creatures

Non-human entities that entered through the breach.

Environmental Entities

Reality distortions that interact with the player in unpredictable ways.

Each new enemy type must support the same design principles:

Industrial  
Unsettling  
Unpredictable  
Dangerous
